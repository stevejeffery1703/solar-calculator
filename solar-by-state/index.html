Solar By State

Different geographical locations present different opportunities for solar power, and it's important to plan carefully with these in mind.

Differences in average solar intensity

The most obvious difference between states is the amount and intensity of sunlight they receive. Sunny states with long, cloudless days are among the brightest, with California, Nevada, Arizona, New Mexico, and Texas at the top of the list. But Florida, the Deep South, and parts of the midwest aren't far behind.

Obviously solar intensity varies within each state, especially in large states such as California. Your local specialist supplier will be able to give a realistic estimate of solar power output for your precise location.

Differences in average installation cost

Just as importantly, the average installation cost varies substantially from state to state. This factor is often neglected, which is a mistake, as it has a significant impact on the affordability of solar in many parts of the U.S. Arizona leads the pack here, with Texas and Florida not far behind. But many other states, including the sunny hot-spots of Nevada, North Carolina, California, and Oklahoma also benefit from great value solar installation services.

Differences in state incentives

The final significant geographical difference has to do with state incentives. Depending on where you live, you may be eligible for tax breaks, property tax exemptions, sales tax rebates, cash-back programs, or other financial incentives that bring down the installation cost still further.

These factors depend not only on your state, but also on your county or even your local municipality, and it's therefore not feasible for the SunWise calculator to take account of these automatically. However, if you know about local incentives you can enter the relevant amount manually into the calculator. Crucially, your local installer will be able to give you accurate and up-to-date information.
